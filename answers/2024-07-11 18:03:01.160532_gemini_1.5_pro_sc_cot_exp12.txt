2. Which of the following types of study has the potential to prove cause and effect?
A. Randomized controlled trial B. Qualitative survey C. Case-control D. Cohort 

## Summarizing the Information:

The question asks us to identify the type of study that can demonstrate a cause-and-effect relationship. We are given four options:

* **A. Randomized controlled trial:**  Participants are randomly assigned to different groups (e.g., treatment and control) to test the effect of an intervention.
* **B. Qualitative survey:**  Collects non-numerical data, like opinions and experiences, to understand a topic in-depth.
* **C. Case-control:**  Compares individuals with a specific outcome (cases) to similar individuals without that outcome (controls) to identify potential risk factors.
* **D. Cohort:**  Follows a group of people over time to observe the development of an outcome of interest.

## Determining the Answer:

To establish cause and effect, a study needs to demonstrate that:

1. **The cause precedes the effect in time.**
2. **Changes in the cause are associated with changes in the effect.**
3. **Other plausible explanations for the relationship have been ruled out.**

Out of the given options, only **randomized controlled trials (RCTs)** fulfill these criteria through:

* **Random assignment:**  Minimizes bias and ensures that groups are comparable except for the intervention being tested.
* **Control group:** Provides a baseline for comparison to isolate the effect of the intervention.

## Final Answer:

<answer>A</answer>

* **A. Processing:** This involves various methods to preserve food, which can alter its nutritional content.  Sodium is often added as a preservative, and minerals like potassium can be lost during processing.
* **B. Enrichment:** This is the process of adding back nutrients that were lost during processing, typically focusing on specific vitamins and minerals. It doesn't explain the higher sodium.
* **C. Fortification:** This involves adding nutrients that weren't originally present in the food to increase its nutritional value. It's not relevant to the sodium and potassium difference.
* **D. Additives:** While some additives are preservatives (like salt), this term is too broad. Processing encompasses the addition of salt.

**Conclusion:**

The most likely reason for the higher sodium and lower potassium in canned corn compared to fresh corn is the **processing** itself.
